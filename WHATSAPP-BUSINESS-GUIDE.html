Section 07

Broadcast lists (not groups).

Groups are chaotic. Broadcast lists let us send a single message to up to 256 creators — each receives it as a private message, no one sees the others. This is how we announce new deals without spam.

Lists to create

1
🍽️ Food creatorsAll approved creators who picked "food & cafes" as a category.
2
💇 Beauty & fashionSalons, fashion, skincare creators.
3
🏋️ Fitness & wellnessGyms, trainers, wellness.
4
🏬 All active brandsBrands with at least 1 deal live or closed in last 90 days.
5
⭐ Top 50 creatorsHighest-rated, most reliable. First dibs on premium deals.

Broadcast etiquette

  • Max 2 broadcasts per week. More = people mute the number.
  • Always start with context. "Hey Priya — new deal in your city that matches your style." Never plain "🔥 NEW DEAL".
  • Include a clear CTA. "Reply 'in' to apply" or "Tap here: [link]".
  • Recipients must have saved our number — otherwise the broadcast won't deliver. Always end the signup flow with "Save +91 88156 21916 as Fluence — we'll DM you live deals."

Section 09

Chat hygiene & SLA rules.

The fastest way to kill a WhatsApp business is to reply slowly or inconsistently. Here's the standard we hold ourselves to.

Response time SLAs

WhoTargetMax
🔥 Hot lead (brand)< 5 mins30 mins
✨ New creator signup< 30 mins2 hours
💬 Active deal thread< 15 mins1 hour
❓ General question< 2 hoursSame business day
⏸ Waiting on themFollow up at 48 hrsMax 3 follow-ups then drop

Tone rules

  • Short sentences. Line breaks between ideas. Nothing looks worse than a wall of text.
  • Match their energy. If they type "hey bro", we type casual. If they're formal, we stay formal.
  • No "Dear Sir/Madam". No "Please find attached". We're not a 2012 PSU.
  • Use emoji sparingly — 1 per message max, usually at start for punctuation.
  • Always end with a next step. "Want me to send the link?" beats "let me know".

Hard no's

  • Never argue in writing. Move to a call.
  • Never share one creator's rate/details with another brand or creator.
  • Never promise a specific deal or amount before the brand confirms.
  • Never use the Fluence number for personal chats. Ever.
Section 10

When (and why) to upgrade to WhatsApp API.

Business App is perfect for 0–500 active chats a month. Past that, we need the WhatsApp Business Platform (API) to automate and scale.

Signals we've outgrown the free app

  • More than ~50 inbound messages per day
  • Needing to send more than 2 broadcasts a week to the same creators
  • Wanting to auto-send deal notifications when a new deal is posted in the admin panel
  • Needing 2+ people answering chats from the same number simultaneously
  • Running CRM-style sequences (drip reminders, re-engagement)

ImportantOnce we move to the API, the WhatsApp Business App on the phone STOPS WORKING for this number. It's one-way. Pick the right BSP the first time — migration back is painful.

What we'd build on API (future)

  • Auto-welcome from the admin panel → new creator gets a DM the moment they submit the signup form.
  • Deal alerts — when a brand posts a deal in category X, push a template message to all matching creators.
  • Status updates — "Your application was approved / post is live / payment sent" all auto-triggered from the Google Sheet.
  • Two-factor auth for creator login via WhatsApp OTP (cheaper than SMS long-term).
When to seriously consider itAround month 4-6 — when we have 300+ creators and 10+ active brands. Start a trial on AiSensy's free tier to test.
